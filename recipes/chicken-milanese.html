Chicken Milanese Recipe

What is the Chicken Milanese?

Chicken Milanese is an exquisite dish that embodies simplicity and elegance in Italian cuisine. It starts with a tender chicken breast, pounded thin to ensure even cooking and tenderness. The chicken is then coated in a breadcrumb mixture, often seasoned with herbs like parsley and grated Parmesan cheese, adding a delightful richness and depth of flavor.

Next, the chicken is lightly fried in olive oil or butter until the coating turns golden brown and crispy, creating a crunchy exterior that contrasts beautifully with the juicy, tender meat inside. The frying process locks in moisture, ensuring each bite is succulent and flavorful.

Once perfectly cooked, the Chicken Milanese is typically served with a garnish of fresh arugula, dressed in a zesty vinaigrette or simply drizzled with lemon juice. This adds a refreshing and peppery element that balances the richness of the chicken.

The dish is often accompanied by lemon wedges, allowing diners to add a citrus-like brightness according to their preference. It's a dish that harmonizes textures and flavors—crispy and tender, savory and tangy—all coming together in a delightful and satisfying culinary experience.

Ingredients

  • Chicken Breasts
  • Breadcrumbs
  • Parmesan Cheese
  • Eggs
  • Flour
  • Herbs and Seasonings
  • Oil and Butter
  • Fresh Arugula
  • Lemon

Preparation & Steps

  1. Prepare the Chicken: Place the chicken breasts between two sheets of plastic wrap and pound them to an even thickness (about 1/4 to 1/2 inch thick). Season both sides of the chicken breasts with salt and pepper.

  2. Set Up the Dredging Station: In three separate shallow bowls or plates, set up a dredging station. Place flour in one, beaten eggs in another, and a mixture of breadcrumbs, grated Parmesan, chopped parsley, salt, and pepper in the third bowl.

  3. Coat the Chicken: Dredge each chicken breast in the flour, shaking off any excess. Then dip it into the beaten eggs, allowing any excess to drip off. Finally, coat the chicken thoroughly in the breadcrumb mixture, pressing gently to adhere the crumbs.

  4. Fry the Chicken: Heat a generous amount of olive oil or vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the coated chicken breasts (you may need to cook them in batches to avoid overcrowding the pan). Cook for about 3-4 minutes on each side or until golden brown and cooked through. Transfer the cooked chicken to a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.

  5. Serve: Arrange a bed of fresh arugula on plates. Place the Chicken Milanese on top of the arugula. Garnish with lemon wedges.
